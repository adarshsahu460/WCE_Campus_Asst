# Walchand College of Engineering, Sangli
## Academic Regulations for Bachelor of Engineering (B.E.)
### Academic Year 2023-2024

---

## 1. Admission and Registration

### 1.1 Eligibility
- Candidates must have passed 10+2 or equivalent examination with Physics, Chemistry, and Mathematics.
- Minimum 50% aggregate marks (45% for reserved categories).
- Valid JEE Main score or state-level entrance examination score.

### 1.2 Registration
- All students must register at the beginning of each semester.
- Late registration attracts a penalty as per university norms.
- Students failing to register within the stipulated time may be deregistered.

### 1.3 Document Verification
- Original documents must be submitted during admission.
- Provisional admission is granted pending document verification.
- Failure to produce original documents leads to cancellation of admission.

---

## 2. Attendance Requirements

### 2.1 Minimum Attendance
- Minimum 75% attendance is mandatory for all courses.
- Attendance is calculated separately for theory and practical components.
- Medical leave with valid documentation may be considered for attendance shortage.

### 2.2 Attendance Shortage
- Students with attendance below 75% but above 65% may be allowed to appear for exams with the permission of the HOD.
- Attendance below 65% leads to debarment from end-semester examinations.
- Debarred students must re-register for the course in the next semester.

### 2.3 Special Provisions
- Students representing the college in sports/cultural events get duty leave.
- Industrial visits and academic tours are counted as attendance.
- Maximum of 10 days medical leave can be adjusted per semester.

---

## 3. Examination Rules

### 3.1 Continuous Assessment
- Continuous assessment constitutes 20% of the total marks.
- Components: Class tests (10%), Assignments (5%), Quizzes (5%).
- Minimum 50% attendance in continuous assessment activities is required.

### 3.2 Mid-Semester Examination
- Conducted after 7-8 weeks of instruction.
- Duration: 1.5 hours
- Weightage: 20% of total marks
- Syllabus: First 50% of the course content

### 3.3 End-Semester Examination
- Conducted at the end of each semester.
- Duration: 3 hours
- Weightage: 40% of total marks
- Covers entire syllabus

### 3.4 Practical Examination
- Conducted at the end of each semester.
- Weightage: 20% of total marks
- Components: Lab work (10%), Practical exam (10%)

### 3.5 Passing Criteria
- Minimum 40% in theory (combined CA + MSE + ESE)
- Minimum 40% in practical component
- Minimum 40% overall in the course

---

## 4. Grading System

### 4.1 Grade Points

| Grade | Marks Range | Grade Points |
|-------|-------------|--------------|
| O | 90-100 | 10 |
| A+ | 80-89 | 9 |
| A | 70-79 | 8 |
| B+ | 60-69 | 7 |
| B | 55-59 | 6 |
| C | 50-54 | 5 |
| P | 40-49 | 4 |
| F | Below 40 | 0 |

### 4.2 CGPA Calculation
CGPA = Σ(Credit × Grade Point) / Σ(Credit)

### 4.3 Percentage Equivalent
Percentage = (CGPA - 0.5) × 10

---

## 5. Backlog and Reappearing

### 5.1 Supplementary Examination
- Students failing in any course can appear in supplementary exams.
- Supplementary exams are conducted once per semester.
- Maximum 4 attempts per course (including regular attempt).

### 5.2 Improvement
- Students can improve grades by reappearing in end-semester exams.
- Only ESE component can be improved.
- Best of the two attempts is considered.

### 5.3 Academic Probation
- Students with CGPA below 5.0 are placed on academic probation.
- Probation students must clear backlogs within one year.
- Failure to clear probation leads to year-back.

---

## 6. Leave and Break of Study

### 6.1 Semester Break
- A student can take a break for one or more semesters.
- Prior approval from the Dean is required.
- Maximum break period: 2 years

### 6.2 Year Back
- Students with more than 6 backlogs cannot be promoted.
- Year back students must repeat the entire year.
- Fees applicable as per current rates.

---

## 7. Anti-Ragging Policy

### 7.1 Definition
Ragging includes any act that causes physical or psychological harm to a student.

### 7.2 Penalties
- First offense: Suspension for one semester
- Second offense: Expulsion from the institution
- Criminal cases may be filed as per law

### 7.3 Reporting
- Anti-ragging helpline: 1800-XXX-XXXX
- Complaints can be filed with the Anti-Ragging Committee
- All complaints are kept confidential

---

## 8. Code of Conduct

### 8.1 Academic Integrity
- Plagiarism is strictly prohibited.
- Copying in exams leads to cancellation of the paper.
- Repeat offenders may be expelled.

### 8.2 Dress Code
- Students must wear ID cards at all times.
- Formal dress is required during presentations and vivas.
- Laboratory dress code must be followed.

### 8.3 Use of Mobile Phones
- Mobile phones must be switched off during classes.
- Use of mobile phones in exams is considered malpractice.
- Phones may be used in designated areas only.

---

## 9. Fee Structure

### 9.1 Tuition Fee
- Fee structure is as per government norms.
- Fees are payable at the beginning of each semester.
- Late payment attracts a fine of ₹50 per day.

### 9.2 Refund Policy
- Full refund if withdrawal within 7 days of admission.
- 80% refund within 30 days.
- No refund after 30 days.
